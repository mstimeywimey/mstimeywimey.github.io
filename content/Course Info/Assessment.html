Mathematics, Primary and Junior, Part One

Assessment and Evaluation

Grading Framework

A range of methods for assessing student achievement will be used across all ETFO courses. These methods will include both formative and summative assessments.

Assessment of assignments will involve three perspectives:

  • Self assessment
  • Peer feedback
  • Course instructor assessment

Formal and informal feedback mechanisms used by instructors during the course to inform candidates about their progress will include:

  • Written feedback
  • Use of rubrics and checklists

Course candidates are expected to work cooperatively by sharing experiences, knowledge, ideas and resources, and by providing support to each other in small and large discussion forums. Standards for successful completion of each course will be based on the following criteria:

  • Regular online attendance in accordance with course schedule;
  • Contributes to class discussions and activities;
  • Completes all course assignments meeting all of the criteria outlined, before or on the due date; and
  • Meets overall course expectations.

Candidates will be required to keep an electronic portfolio that they will use to organize their assignments. This portfolio will be shared with the instructor for feedback.

The following grading framework will be used by instructors to guide assessment and evaluation of assignments in these courses. Candidates who are unsuccessful with specific assignments will be provided with opportunities to resubmit the assignment after it has been revised based upon the instructor feedback.

In order to successfully complete a course and be recommended to the Ontario College of Teachers for the appropriate notation, the participant must pass all of the criteria.

Assessment of Summative Tasks

Summative Task Assignments will be submitted at the end of each Learning Experience for assessment and feedback from the Instructor.

Connections: Candidate applies theory and professional practice to summative task:
  • Clear links to theory with reference to course readings in summative task.
  • Connections to class discussions are evident.
  • Includes a plan for sharing the summative task at a later date.

Successful completion of this course is not automatic. Where there is cause to consider not recommending a participant, this will be communicated to the candidates well in advance with steps taken to provide support. ETFO believes very strongly that there should be no surprises regarding academic achievement – candidates who are at risk of being unsuccessful in the course will be informed of this as soon as concerns are evident and supports will be put in place to assist them.
